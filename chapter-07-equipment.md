This chapter covers all varieties of new items, armors, weapons and tools you may use. If an item is marked with an asterix ( * ), see its description for special rules.

**Note regarding item size and object size:** All equipment listed in this chapter has a size. This 'item size' indicates how easy each item is to use and conceal and carries some clue about its general dimensions. An item's size is two sizes larger than it's object size, and any item with an item size equal to your size (or smaller) can be handled with one hand. Generally speaking, object size is similar to creature size. (Medium object size is roughly the same as the size as a Medium creature. That means an item listed as Huge in this chapter is roughly the same size as a Medium creature)

**Size:** The size of an item.

**Weight:** The item's weight. Altering a weapon's size doubles its weight (for each size increase) or halves its weight (for each size decrease).

**Cost:** The DC of the Wealth check you must succeed at to buy the item.

**Res:** The restriction rating of the item (if any) and its purchase DC modifier to acquire it on the black market.

## General Equipment

### Accessories

**Adrenaline Boost:** Comes in a ready-to-use syringe. As a move-equivalent action, you can inject yourself, gaining a +2 bonus to Strength and Dexterity for one hour, during which you can also act as normal while below 0 hit points. Once the effect ends, you become *fatigued* and suffer 1 temporary Constitution damage.

**Chakra Restoration Amplifier:** This potion, often jokingly referred to as the 'crap' (Chakra Restoration Amplifying Potion) can force your body to produce Chakra. For 1d4+1 rounds, you will recover 2 points of Chakra per round, which cannot exceed your maximum Chakra. While this effect lasts, you take a -8 penalty to all Chakra Control checks. If you ingest a CRAP while another is still in effect, reduce its duration by 2 rounds (this penalty stacks if you ingest another before the second one ends).

If you consume more craps in 24 hours than your Intelligence modifier (max 3), your Chakra recovery from all sources is halved for 2 days. If you consume at least twice as much, this penalty lasts for 2 weeks instead.

**Chakra Restoration Amplifier, Greater:** As its normal counterpart, but you recover 4 points of Chakra per round instead of 2, the penalty to Chakra Control checks increases to -15, and you cannot tap your reserves while the GCRAP is in effect. If you have fewer than 10 hit dice, you suffer 4d6 damage each round the chemical is in effect.

**Chakra Storing Gem:** These gemstones are covered in tiny seals, ready to absorb and release Chakra. Chakra in these gems is undetectable to creatures Sensing Chakra, though it glows brilliantly to those who can see Chakra. Once per day per gem, you can make a Chakra Control check (DC 25) to pour any amount of Chakra into a gem as a full-round action. Failure by less than 5 allows you to proceed, but you spend 50% more Chakra if you do so. You can draw from a Chakra gem's stores by making skin contact with it just before or after performing a technique. You can only draw Chakra from Chakra storing gems once every 24 hours (no matter which gem was used).

After you draw Chakra from any Chakra gem, that gem can no longer be filled with Chakra. When a gem's stores are emptied, it crumbles to dust. A Chakra storing gem can hold 5 (Least), 10 (Lesser) or 20 (Greater) Chakra.

**Perception Oil:** This vial of rare, translucent oil can be applied to the skin. For 1d4+1 hours, you suffer a -1 penalty to Search, Spot, and Survival checks, attack rolls and Defense. In return, you gain a 50 ft. range increment to your Sense Chakra ability, and a 15 ft. increase to your Dormant Senses. You also gain a +3 bonus to Spot and Wisdom checks made while using Sense Chakra. A single dose is enough to cover one Medium or Small creature, or two Tiny or smaller ones.

**Utility Pills:** These pills cover various purposes a field operative routinely needs, and take effect immediately.

*Blood Clotting Pill:* You heal 1 hit point, and gain a +2 bonus to Fort saves against massive damage for 1d4+1 rounds. Anyone attempting to restore your hit points with the Treat Injury skill restores an additional 2 hit points. If you consume more than one pill within an hour, you must make a Fort save (DC 20, +1 per extra pill) or become *nauseated* for 1 minute.

*Ration Pill:* You will not suffer from the effects of *Starvation and Thirst* for 6 days (144 hours). Once the effect ends, you suffer 1d4 temporary Con damage. Eating three full meals and resting a full day heals this damage. Ration pills can only be consumed if you've eaten and drunk as normal for at least a week beforehand.

*Sense Tablet:* For 1d4+1 hours, you gain a +1 bonus to Listen, Search, Spot and Survival checks, as well as a +4 bonus to saves against sleep effects. The bonus to skill checks improves by +1 every hour until the drug's effects end. If you can Sense Chakra, increase its range by 50%. Once the effect ends, you suffer 1d2 temporary Wisdom damage and become *nauseated* for 1 hour (Fort save DC 18 negates the nausea). You can counter the nausea by taking another tablet, though doing so will increase both the effect of subsequent nausea by 2 hours and increase the Wisdom damage by 1 for every extra tablet ingested past the first. Extending the duration of the pills like this continues to increase the skill bonuses, up to +10. Characters under prolonged effects become increasingly violent and irritating/jittery as time passes.

If the Wisdom damage taken is higher than your Wisdom score, make a save against massive damage to

*Soldier Pill:* You gain 2d4+1 points of temporary Chakra lasting 72 hours. You can ignore *Starvation and Thirst* for 24 hours and gain a +4 bonus to saves against sleep effects for 24 hours, or until the temporary Chakra runs out. Furthermore, the penalties of being *exhausted* are reduced to -4, and you can still run or charge while *fatigued* or *exhausted*, though only at three times your speed. Finally, you gain a +4 bonus to Con checks to keep running. Once the effect fades, you will be *nauseated* for 2d6+2 hours (Fort save DC 15 for half). For 36 hours after consuming the pill, you are unable to sleep, rest or naturally recover hit points and/or Chakra.

The effects of these pills do not stack or refresh each other. If you are knocked unconscious while under the effect of a soldier pill, the effect immediately fades (and you become nauseated, as above).

### Books and Scrolls

**Blank Scroll:** Most commonly used for sealing techniques or to create Summoning Scrolls or a Scroll of Seals. This scroll can be filled with up to 10 pages of text, each page containing up to 250 words. A short scroll can only contain up to 5 pages, while a long scroll can carry up to 100 pages.

**Elemental Scroll:** These scrolls are bought of a specific element, and carry 10 charges. When performing a technique of an element matching the scroll, you may spend one charge and increase the perform time by one step to gain a +2 circumstance bonus to meet the perform requirements. In addition, you count as one level higher for that technique. Once all charges have been expended, the scroll becomes a Blank Scroll.

**Encyclopedia Shinobi:** This set of six volumes details a lot of information about almost every aspect of shinobi life. A new version is released every 20 years, making previous versions obsolete. These encyclopedias go into great detail about their subjects, including descriptions, historical references and many useful facts (see below for specifics).

*Volume I – Ninja Tools and Resources:* Contains an introduction to the encyclopedia, and information about various weapons, tools, attributes and chemicals used in the shinobi world. It details the various uses and components of both puppets and traps, and names any famous users or builders of such. The volume also contains a glance at various famous artifacts, with some hints about their history, locations and (previous) owners.

*Volume II – Tales of the Great Wars:* Goes into great detail about the more famous ninjas and summoned creatures used in past wars. If you know a description of a creature or person who took part in the war, a Research check (DC 10 + the subject's total hit dice at that time) will reveal basic information about their abilities and achievements during the war.

*Volume III – The Continent:* Described within is a great deal of information about the topography of the continent, with maps and information on roads and many cities, as well as an approximation of the major hidden villages. It will hold descriptions, maps and pictures of various ninja-specific landmarks.

*Volume IV – The Clans and their Powers:* The many ninja clans around the world are the subject of this volume, describing notable figures within such clans and possibly information on the more famous bloodlines. You can make a Research check (DC 20) to discover general information about a clan. A Research check (DC 30) can reveal information about specific clan members or bloodlines, so long as they were part of recorded instances.

*Volume V – Chakra and the Ninja Arts:* Contains a variety of basic techniques any ninja should have access to. Using this volume grants a +1 Learn bonus when learning Bunshin no Jutsu, Chouyaku no Jutsu, Fukurougan, Henge no Jutsu, Genjutsu Kai, Kawarimo no Jutsu, Taijutsu: Keikai-uchi, Kinobori, Nawanuke no Jutsu, Shougenzou no Jutsu, Tadayou, Takitsuke and Zentai Bougyo.

In addition, each country's edition includes a basic elemental technique: Earth – Dochuu Engyou no Jutsu, Fire – Kaen Shuriken, Lightning – Raishuriken no Jutsu, Water – Mizudama no Jutsu and Wind – Kazegama no Jutsu.

*Volume VI – Indexes:* This volume contains no information; it is merely a large index showing a potential reader where to find information on which subjects within the other five volumes.

**Scroll of Seals:** These scrolls contain enough information about a technique to teach it to others. Such scrolls contain up to three techniques, and can be used to self-teach techniques (see p106), granting a bonus based on who crafted the scroll (see Craft (Calligraphy), p41). The purchase DC of a scroll of seals increases by 1 for every 2 Ranks worth of techniques within it.

*Lesser:* These can usually be purchased in licensed ninja shops. Contains up to 6 total ranks worth of techniques.

*Greater:* As Lesser, but contains up to 14 total ranks. Rarely seen outside of the safes of prominent clans/shinobi.

Some techniques may not be available in a specific village, and most Hijutsu and Kinjutsu can't be bought in public shops. The restriction level is +1 (Licensed) for E- and D-Rank techniques, +2 (Restricted) for C-Rank techniques, +3 (Military) for B- and A-Rank ones and +4 (Illegal) for S-Rank and Super S-Rank techniques.

**Sealing Scroll:** This special scrolls contain inscriptions to augment techniques that store objects (such as *Gesouin* or *Chuuzouin*), allowing each seal to hold twice as much material as normal. A standard scroll holds 5 pages of text or 5 seal slots, and a long scroll holds 50 pages of text or 20 seal slots.

**Summoning Scroll:** These scrolls help to summon creatures, but can only be used once, after which they become a blank scroll. Using these scrolls increases the perform time of a technique by one step.

*Lesser:* You gain a +2 bonus to meet the perform requirements of Summoning techniques, and you count as one level higher to meet summon rank requirements and/or determine the level of creature you can summon.

*Greater:* As Lesser, but the bonus is +4 and you count as two levels higher.

**Technique Scroll:** These unique scrolls are carefully crafted to absorb a single Ninjutsu or Genjutsu technique of rank 10 or lower, which can be unleashed from the scroll at a later time.

When used, form a half-seal and make a Perform check for the technique at a -4 penalty. If successful, you perform the technique normally, ignoring any Hand Seal component it has, but you suffer a -10 penalty to tap your reserves this round. If the technique requires material components, they must be present. If the technique can be empowered, you may empower it up to the level it was empowered when inscribed.

When successfully used, the scroll is discharged. You can recharge it by making a Chakra Control check (DC 20 + technique's rank) and paying half the XP required to craft it (7.5 XP per rank of the technique). Doing so requires 10 minutes.

These scrolls are rarely available for purchase, but when they are the purchase DC is 20 + the technique's rank.

### Containers

**Belt Pouch:** A small pouch which can carry up to 10 pounds of items in three compartments.

**Belt Pouch, Larger:** This larger pouch has 5 compartments and can carry up to 25 pounds of items.

**Preservation Container:** These one-time use containers can store organic matter for 10 days, preventing decay.

*Tiny:* Contains up to half a pound of matter, such as a pair of eyes or a finger.

*Small:* Contains up to one pound of matter, such as a kidney or a heart.

*Medium:* Contains up to 10 pounds of matter, such as a brain or liver.

*Large:* Contains up to 50 pounds of matter, such as a pair of lungs.

**Shuriken Holster:** A small pouch that can be strapped to an arm or leg, it can hold 4 kunai, 10 throwing knives or 15 shuriken or throwing needles. It has a holster to hold a kunai on the outside.

**Shuriken Holster, Large:** A larger version with three compartments, each of which holds as much as a normal shuriken holster. (Only has one holster)

### Clothing

**Conical Hat:** Traditional travelling headwear, like a *sugegasa*. Similar to those often worn by rice paddy workers.

**Forehead Protector:** A piece of cloth with a metal plate identifying your village. These are only handed out at graduation from the academy, and are entirely illegal to sell, buy, craft or possess through other means.

**Ninja Outfits:** These outfits do not hinder the wearer in combat, and usually include: pants/skirt, shirt, undergarments, shoes/sandals/open-toed boots, socks/stockings, belt/suspenders, and sometimes a fishnet undershirt. These clothes do not count against your encumbrance.

*Casual:* Basic clothes used for training or errands.

*Standard:* A standard ninja uniform. Has a color and camouflage based on your village, granting a +2 equipment bonus to Hide checks in those environments (The patterns are: snow, night, forest, plains or rock). You can request a uniform with a pattern that differs from your village's basic one.

**Travel Cloak:** These full-body cloaks have about a dozen pockets that can hold up to 1 lb. of materials in total. Most cloaks have a hood, and can be quickly snapped on or off (removing your cloak is a free action). Can be printed with a pattern, identical in effect to that of a standard ninja outfit.

## Weapons

The weapons are organized by category, based on which Weapon Proficiency feat they belong to.

**Bows and ammunition:** Bows are projectile weapons. When you attack with such weapons, you can draw the ammunition as part of the attack, as long as it is readily available to you (such as being carried in a quiver).

Ammunition can be crafted with the Craft(mechanical) skill as normal, but a single attempt creates twenty pieces. Ammunition is destroyed on hit, and has a 50% chance of being destroyed if it misses.

**Attacking with thrown weapons:** When making attacks with most thrown weapons (such as kunai or shuriken), add your Strength modifier to the damage roll.

To throw a weapon, it must be ready in your hand unless you have the Quick Draw feat. Some weapons may allow you to draw one or more of them freely when attacking.

### Weapon Size

Weapons can be made for creatures of a different size. To do so, adjust the weapon's size accordingly. A Kodachi made for a Large creature, for example, would be a Medium-sized Kodachi. Changing a weapon's size also changes its weapon damage (see the table below for examples of weapon damage, based on the creature size it was made for). Increasing an item one size category roughly doubles its weight.

A character can wield weapons made for a different size character, but its effort changes based on its size in relation to you: a weapon of the same size as you is a one-handed weapon, and a weapon one size smaller or larger than you is considered a light or two-handed weapon (respectively).

If a weapon's size causes its effort to become less than light or more than two-handed, you can't use it. For each size category you differ from a weapon's intended wielder, you suffer a -2 penalty to attack rolls.

Special materials will modify this value, and may confer some special abilities to items crafted out of them. If a weapon's damaging parts can't be crafted out of metal, these materials confer no benefit. When buying or crafting a double weapon, you can use a different material for each head, although doing so adds the purchase modifier of both materials to the base weapon.

**Cold Iron:** This iron is forged at low temperatures and is mined deep underground. Applying an enhancement seal to a cold iron weapon increases the cost by 2.

**Dark Iron:** This heavy iron is mined deep within the earth. When sundering weapons or attacking objects, ignore any hardness of 15 or lower.

**Heavy Dark Iron:** This material is extremely rare and heavy. Weapons made of this material count as though one size larger than usual, but only for the purpose of the effort required to wield it. When sundering, ignore

ignore any hardness of 20 or lower. The critical hit multiplier of such weapons increases by 1. Crafting these items takes six times the normal amount of time, and crafting these items without a heavy dark iron tool kit incurs a -8 penalty to the craft check.

**Ironium:** This material confers no special benefits to weapons crafted with it beyond hardness and hit points.

**Nonlethal:** These weapons are made of wood, rubber and/or plastic and deal nonlethal damage without penalty, but trying to deal lethal damage with them incurs a -4 penalty to attacks. A nonlethal weapon's critical hit multiplier is always 2. Certain weapons may not be available as nonlethal versions, such as explosives and firearms.

**Silver:** These weapons confer a -1 penalty to damage rolls.

**Thorium:** This silvery-gold metal is quite rare and can help to focus Chakra more easily, but does not confer any special benefits to a weapon.

**Oversized:** These weapons are crafted to be larger than normal. Such a weapon is counted as one size larger in all respects, though it does not deal increased damage. If you can treat the weapon as a two-handed weapon, it gains reach. If it already has reach, its damage die increases by one step.

**Paper Bomb:** These staple items are pieces of paper covered in sealing arrays, and are also known as exploding tags. Paper bombs come with an adhesive on their back, allowing them to stick to surfaces.

They can be attached to a surface, attached to a thrown weapon (if the weapon hits, the target takes a -4 penalty to its save against the explosion) or directly attached to a creature by succeeding at a grapple attempt (or grapple check if already grappling; if successful, the creature does not get a save against the explosion).

*Charging:* As a move action while touching a paper bomb, you can add up to five charges to it, which requires a Chakra Control Check, the base DC for which depends on the type of paper bomb used (see below). Adding more than one charge costs 1 Chakra per additional charge, and increases the DC for the Chakra Control check by 1 per additional charge. Once a paper bomb has at least one charge, it is *charged* for 1 minute per character level.

*Detonating:* To detonate a paper bomb, it must be *charged*. You can either set a timed delay of up to 1 minute when charging a paper bomb (which increases the Chakra Control DC by 5) to let it detonate after the selected time, or you can set off a paper bomb within 50 ft. of you as an instant action. You can only set off bombs if you have the same Chakra Signature as the creature who charged the bomb, and you must know its approximate location.

*Linking:* It is possible to link up to 1 paper bomb per level together, as long as they are all charged by creatures with the same Chakra Signature, and are all the same type of paper bomb. One *charged* bomb will be the primary bomb, and you can spend an attack action to add a second *charged* tag, placing it within 10 ft. of the primary bomb. A link is broken when one of its bombs is moved or loses its charge.

Linked bombs are set off all at once by detonating one of the linked bombs, creating one giant explosion centered on the primary bomb. The explosion is centered around the primary bomb, which will explode as normal, but its damage increases by 1 charge per linked bomb, which can exceed the normal charge limit, and its blast radius increases by 5 ft. per linked bomb (up to a limit, see below).

Alternatively, you can create a 'long link'. Such a link functions like normal, but instead of all connecting to a primary bomb, you create a long chain where bombs can be linked at any distance, as long as they are placed within the blast radius of an 'adjacent' bomb (based on its explosion radius after the new bomb been linked). Linked bombs only gain increased damage and explosion radius for each other bomb 'adjacent' to them in the link.

When it strikes a hard surface (or is struck hard), it releases a cloud of poison covering a 10ft cube. Any creature within the area is affected (holding one's breath is ineffective against inhaled poisons).

*Chakra Mantle:* If a creature with a Chakra Pool of 10 or lower is affected, it must save against 2d4 hours of unconsciousness.

*Ghostly Chains:* If a target fails both saves, it also gains a -1 stacking speed rank penalty, which counts as Strength damage for medical techniques and natural healing.

## Shinobi Drugs

These drugs are high-grade pharmaceuticals that can permanently enhance a user's physical or mental performance, but they are rare and difficult to acquire. The price of these drugs may vary between suppliers.

### Taking Shinobi Drugs

When you ingest a shinobi drug, you must immediately make a Fortitude save against its initial damage. One minute after ingesting it, you must make a second Fortitude save, this time against its secondary damage. Every drug you have taken permanently increases the DC of future saves against shinobi drugs by 1.

Save bonuses against poisons or diseases apply to saves against drug effects, though you may apply only one of those. Drugs imbibed while immune to poisons and/or diseases have no effect (positive or negative) on you.

A drug's positive effects will come into effect once any damage suffered by the drug is fully healed. If no damage was taken, it will take effect immediately after you succeed at your save against the secondary damage. Damage taken by failing saves can only be healed by characters of a level higher than half the save DC, Medical techniques that specifically heal poison or disease-based damage cannot be used to reverse the damage taken from drugs.
